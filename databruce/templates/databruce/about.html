Welcome to Databruce, a new Bruce Springsteen database.

What is Databruce?

Databruce is a database for tracking stats about Bruce Springsteen's performing history. It was created by and is maintained by lilbud.

DB was first made available to a small group of friends in June-July 2025 in a sort of "beta testing" phase while I made sure everything was working, as well as to get feedback on the site itself. While the site has been "open to the public" since the middle of July, the testing phase more or less continued while I worked on the site to get it to a state I'd consider "almost just exactly perfect".

Also I rewrote this page at least half a dozen times and that took a bit of time as well, but anyway, on with the show.

Why Create It?

I created DB after being inspired by several newer database sites for other bands, sites like Jerrybase, ElGoose, Dripfield.pro, and others. I really liked the layout and especially the features provided by each of them. Jerrybase in particular was a big influence with its advanced search features, which I'm not aware of many "artist database" sites having.

The only Bruce-focused database site with a search feature even close to the above is one called "Killing Floor" (developed by Paolo Calvi). It has a basic event search, allowing location and setlist searching. However, the site hasn't received an update since January 2019, and is unlikely to get one.

I started compiling the database in early 2023 for use with Brucebot (Discord bot, another project of mine.) While the bot is good, it has the downside of being somewhat limited in terms of it's features and capabilities. It is only capable of very simple lookups, anything more advanced would require using the underlying SQL database, which isn't very accessible for most people. In creating Databruce, my intention was to create an easier and user-friendly way to interact with that database.

What Sets It Apart?

Databruce was created to be a more "modern" Bruce database site, I took bits and pieces that I liked from other sites and created something that fills a niche that I felt none of the current sites did. I saw the kind of efforts that other communities have managed, and wanted to create something like them for Bruce.

DB's features are what set it apart. I created something that more closely resembles the sites mentioned in the previous section. A site that allowed more than just "viewing" the data (like Brucebase), but allowed users to interact with the data like a proper database would allow, but without the barrier to entry that something like a Postgres database would have.

To be clear, I'd like to say that I am not acting like Databruce is better than Brucebase. Throughout this page I will be comparing Databruce to Brucebase, simply because it is the primary Bruce database site out there right now, least of those that are current. Their site (along with others) was the primary inspiration and source for much of the data shown on this site. Without them and the work they've done over the decades, there would be no Databruce, its that simple.

Unique Features

This section will briefly cover some of the features that Databruce provides. This is by no means comprehensive, but more of an "overview" I suppose.

General Layout

Taking a page from the sites I mentioned, I tried to create a site that looked better visually, was more up-to-date and inline with the sites I was inspired by. One thing all of them have in common is that they're all very well designed, and present their data in an easy to understand way, and I tried to do the same with Databruce. This is not to say that sites like Brucebase look bad or don't present their data well, but I think most would agree that it is somewhat dated visually. That isn't necessarily a bad thing, just something worth noting.

Searching/Filtering

On many of the pages, the data is presented in Datatables. Which allow for sorting/filtering/searching the data presented, as well as a basic "advanced" search which allows users to filter the displayed data in numerous ways.

Advanced Search

This is something right from Jerrybase. I thought it was an incredibly useful feature that I'm not aware of many "artist database" sites having. Although while developing it I began to understand why, as handling all the possible cases was very tricky. While the concept is very much the same as JB, I believe the execution is different due to differences in the sites backend. Using the advanced search, events can be searched for by many different parameters. Those include:

  • Date Range: events in a specific range of dates. This can be specific days or just months, with the "start" date using the first of the given month, and end using the end of the given month.
  • Month/Day: events on any given day in history. Or even events on a specific day of the week.
  • Location: events at a specific venue or a specific city/state/country.
  • Band/Musicians: find events where a specific person or band appeared on stage with Bruce.

Additionally, there is also a setlist searching feature, which can be used on it's own or in combination with any of the above queries. This allows finding shows where specific songs were played, and even those where two songs were played in a specific order (like Incident > Rosie). The search feature is incredibly powerful, and the possibilities are quite possibly limited. It's also quote possibly pretty broken, as I can't possibly check every possible combination of queries, so keep that in mind.

Setlist Info/Data

This extends to most of the data presented, but I'll just focus on the setlists specifically.

I've taken something of a different approach to the setlists. Again influenced by other sites like I've said. Setlists are broken up by set, and things like snippets, guests, sign requests, and more are tracked as part of the setlists. Which means those stats are data that can be filtered/searched/queried, rather than just appearing as a text-based note on a page.

User Accounts

MyBossTime but faster updates. Users can add shows to their profile, and view stats about the songs they've seen (and haven't seen, my condolences to the Europeans who haven't gotten Rosie)

Credits/Closing

Before this page gets too long (and believe me it was longer), I'd like to close. But first, some thanks are in order. This list is by no means "complete" or "comprehensive". At some point I plan on pulling together a more comprehensive credits list.

First and foremost, Brucebase. This site was largely built upon their data and information, which they've tirelessly gathered over the years. They are an invaluable resource, and I'll say again and again that without them, this site would not exist.

SpringsteenLyrics. This site provided the information on the many bootleg recordings that are out there. Although the data was pretty heavily modified, using my collection to fill in gaps or add additional info.

Jerrybase, ElGoose, Dripfield.pro. I mention all three because they were a source of inspiration for many parts of Databruce. From layout to features to presentation of data. Jerrybase in particular deserves particular thanks, as their advanced search was one of the things that inspired me to create Databruce in the first place. My advanced search is pretty much a copy of theirs, and I used theirs to figure out (on a conceptual level) how it should all work. The setlist search rows was almost entirely influenced by them, as I had no idea where to even begin with figuring it out. ElGoose and Dripfield were a help more on the layout/presentation side of things, as I like the design of both sites.

Lastly, I want to thank the members of the r/brucespringsteen Discord server for being my unwilling ~~Guinea Pigs~~ beta testers. Databruce marks the first time I've done anything on this scale at all (let alone something that was to be publicly available). Their help in testing and providing feedback/ideas was crucial to this site being in the state that it is.

This site is always going to be a work in progress. If you have suggestions/questions, I can usually be found on Discord (.lilbud), or Twitter (@_lilbud_). Feel free to reach out!
